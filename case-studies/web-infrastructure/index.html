1. The Executive Summary: From Bottleneck to Self-Serve

In a hyper-growth startup, Marketing needs to move at the speed of culture, but Engineering is often locked in product sprints. At Eka Care, our web presence was stifled by this dependency—simple copy changes took days, and new campaign pages took weeks.

The Core Innovation

I didn't just "redesign the website"; I architected a Decentralized Publishing Engine. By migrating to Webflow and integrating an AI-assisted development workflow, I shifted the website from an "Engineering Ticket" to a "Design-Led Operation," reducing campaign Turnaround Time (TAT) by 80% and giving Marketing 100% autonomy.

The Challenge: The "Engineering Wall"

The existing website was a hard-coded React app. While performant, it was operationally broken for a marketing team:

Zero Autonomy

Marketing couldn't fix a typo without a developer PR.

Slow-to-Market

Launching a "Hackathon" landing page required pulling engineers off core product work.

The "Bandwidth" Trap

As the sole designer, I risked becoming the new bottleneck—replacing "waiting for devs" with "waiting for design."

The Goal

Build a system that allows high-fidelity design and instant publishing, without requiring a single hour of core engineering time.

Reflection: The "Senior" Take

"This project wasn't about learning Webflow; it was about Resource Allocation. By removing the website from the engineering backlog, I didn't just build a site—I gave the engineering team their time back. I proved that with the right 'No-Code + AI' stack, a design team can own the entire funnel from Figma to Production."
— Kunal Katre, Web Principal & Design Engineer

Key Learnings

  • Operational Thinking > Technical Mastery. The biggest impact came from solving resource allocation, not just technical implementation.
  • No-Code Doesn't Mean No-Architecture. The Client-First system I implemented ensures long-term maintainability and prevents future bottlenecks.
  • AI as a Design Multiplier. Using AI tools strategically (not as a replacement) can compress development cycles without sacrificing quality.
  • Self-Serve Systems Scale. Teaching the content team to publish independently created sustainable operational velocity.
