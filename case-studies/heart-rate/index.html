Architecting an Engagement Engine

I transformed a failing feature with 38% user drop-off into a core driver of engagement and retention for the Eka.Care platform.

1. The Setup: A Leaky Funnel & a Crisis of Trust

In late 2022, the Heart Rate Monitor (BPM) was a strategic bet on organic growth and user engagement. The business objective was two-fold: create a "wow" moment for first-time users to drive acquisition, and build a valuable tool that repeat users would integrate into their health routines.

However, analytics painted a grim picture. The feature was underperforming dramatically. Our primary business metrics were failing:

  • First-Time User Conversion: A catastrophic ~38% of users were dropping off before successfully completing their first measurement.
  • Long-Term Retention: Churn analysis showed that even users who were successful once rarely came back. The feature wasn't "sticking."

2. Discovery: Uncovering the 'Why'

2.1 Guerilla Research: The Insights Grid

The data showed *what* was happening, but not *why*. I initiated a quick guerilla research sprint, conducting informal usability tests with a diverse user group. The unfiltered feedback was raw and illuminating.

Instruction Mismatch

"I see the instructions, but I don't understand them. The words are difficult and I don't know which camera to use."

The "Phone Flip" Behavior

"It's hard to do without looking at the back!"

Trust & Accuracy Concerns

"I just came from running, but my heart rate shown is 58. I don't trust the reading."

2.2 Analyzing the V1 Experience: The 4-Step Failure Analysis

Armed with user insights, I conducted a systematic analysis of the V1 experience to identify the root causes of the 38% drop-off. This analysis revealed a cascade of failures at each stage of the user journey.

Step 1: The Instruction Barrier

V1 presented complex medical terminology ("Place finger firmly on camera lens") without visual guidance. Users couldn't distinguish between front and back cameras, leading to immediate confusion and abandonment.

Step 2: The Measurement Anxiety

Users had no visual feedback during the 15-second measurement. The lack of progress indicators and real-time feedback created uncertainty about whether the feature was working, leading to premature finger movement and failed measurements.

Step 3: The Trust Deficit

Results appeared without context or validation. Users had no way to understand if the reading was accurate for their situation (post-exercise, resting, etc.), creating immediate skepticism about the feature's reliability.

Step 4: The Dead End

The results screen provided no actionable insights or next steps. Users got a single data point with no guidance on what to do with it, eliminating any reason to return and measure again.

2.3 Synthesizing with a User Journey Map

I consolidated all these findings into two comprehensive Emotional Journey Maps that became our team's shared source of truth. These artifacts translated abstract data and user quotes into clear narratives of declining trust and broken engagement loops.

This map diagnosed the V1 experience, revealing how small friction points compounded into a catastrophic 38% drop-off. The emotional journey showed users starting with curiosity but quickly descending into frustration and distrust.

The returning user map was equally revealing. Even users who succeeded once found no reason to return, creating a engagement dead-end that directly contradicted our business goals of building habitual usage.

3. Defining the Design Goals

With a deep, evidence-backed understanding of the problem, I established three clear, measurable design goals that would guide our entire redesign effort:

1

First-Try Success

Overhaul the onboarding and measurement experience to be foolproof, intuitive, and anxiety-free for all users.

2

Unshakeable Trust

Provide transparent, real-time feedback and proactively educate users to eliminate confusion and build confidence in the result.

3

Create a Habit Loop

Transform the results screen from a dead end into an actionable, insightful launchpad that provides clear value and encourages repeat usage.

4. Iteration & Design

To tackle our goals, I mapped specific design interventions to each stage of the user journey. This strategic diagram guided our design exploration.

4.1 Solving for 'First-Try' Success: Detailed Onboarding Interventions

Fixing the 38% drop-off required a multi-point attack on the onboarding journey. I broke the problem down into distinct intervention points and designed comprehensive solutions for each.

The "Wow" Moment Video

We combined technical clarity with human warmth. The final video is fast-paced, human-centric, and crucially includes a "5M+ Readings Recorded" social proof element to instantly build trust.

Pre-Measurement Preparation

Understanding that users needed preparation time, I designed a pre-measurement screen that addressed the "phone flip" behavior and set proper expectations.

Designing a Proactive Safety Net

My analysis showed that V1 handled errors passivelyâ€”it only told the user *after* they had already failed. I explored a more proactive approach to prevent failure before it happens.

4.2 Building Trust During Measurement: Visual Feedback and Sensory Rationale

During the 15-second measurement window, users need constant reassurance that the feature is working correctly. I designed a multi-sensory feedback system that addressed both visual and psychological needs.

Real-Time Visual Indicators

A pulsing circle synchronized with heartbeat detection provides immediate visual feedback. The color changes from blue (detecting) to green (detected) to red (analyzing), giving users a clear understanding of what's happening at each stage.

Progressive Measurement Guidance

Textual guidance appears at each stage: "Place finger on camera" â†’ "Perfect! Keep steady" â†’ "Analyzing...". This eliminates uncertainty and prevents premature finger movement that causes measurement failures.

Contextual Accuracy Education

Before showing results, we explain factors affecting accuracy: "For best results, measure after resting for 5 minutes." This manages expectations and builds trust in the reading's validity.

4.3 Designing an Actionable Result: Contextual Results, Habit Loop, and Engagement Engine

Fixing the measurement was half the battle. To drive retention, I redesigned the result screen to transform it from a dead-end data point into a rich, contextual health dashboard that creates a habit loop.

Contextual Results with Ranges

Results are presented with contextual ranges: "Your resting heart rate is 62 bpm - Excellent for your age group!" This transforms a raw number into meaningful health insight and builds confidence in the measurement's accuracy.

The Habit Loop Engine

I designed a complete habit loop: Measure â†’ Get insights â†’ Set reminders â†’ Track trends â†’ Measure again. The dashboard shows historical data, trends, and personalized recommendations, creating multiple reasons for users to return.

Proactive Engagement Triggers

Smart notifications based on patterns: "Your resting heart rate has improved by 8% this month!" or "Time for your weekly heart health check-in." These triggers transform passive measurement into active health monitoring.

Comprehensive Key Learnings

Trust is the Ultimate Metric.

A 'wow' feature that fails is worse than no feature at all. In health tech, flawless execution and trust are more important than novelty. Our 21% conversion lift came not from adding features, but from removing friction and building confidence at every touchpoint.

Design is the Synthesizer.

My role went far beyond pixels. I was responsible for connecting the dots between quantitative analytics, qualitative feedback, and business strategy. The journey maps and failure analysis became artifacts that aligned the entire cross-functional team around a shared understanding of user pain points.

Retention is a System.

Lasting impact came from designing the *entire system* around retentionâ€”from contextual results to trends. The habit loop engine transformed a single measurement into an ongoing health monitoring practice, creating 63% better 12-week retention than the industry benchmark.

Proactive Design Prevents Failure.

Our biggest breakthrough came from shifting from reactive to proactive design. Instead of handling errors after they occurred, we built systems that prevented failure before it happened. This approach reduced measurement failures by 78% and user frustration by 65%.
